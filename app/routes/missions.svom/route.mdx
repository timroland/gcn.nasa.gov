# Space-based multi-band astronomical Variable Objects Monitor (SVOM)

**Launch Date:** June 22, 2024

**Anticipated Start of Science Operations:** February 2025

**End of Operations:** Mission duration of 3 years

The [Space-based multi-band astronomical Variable Objects Monitor (SVOM)](https://www.svom.eu/en/the-svom-mission/) is a French-Chinese mission, result of a collaboration between the two national space agencies, [China National Space Administration (CNSA)](https://www.cnsa.gov.cn/english/index.html) and [Centre national d'études spatiales (CNES)](https://cnes.fr/en/projects/svom). SVOM mission is dedicated to the study of the the most powerful transient phenomena, with a particular emphasis on gamma-ray bursts (GRBs).

The SVOM spacecraft carries four multi-wavelength instruments: ECLAIRs, Gamma Ray burst Monitor (GRM), Microchannel X-ray Telescope (MXT) and Visual Telescope (VT). ECLAIRs and GRM can detect gamma-ray transient sources in real-time with localisation capabilities for ECLAIRs. SVOM can transfer the alerts in near real-time with a typical latency of less than 30s and perform autonomous slew to allow x-ray and optical follow-up of the source with the smaller field of view instruments: MXT and VT.

#### GCN Notice Types in GCN Kafka

SVOM distributes alerts and localizations of gamma-ray burst and other transients. For now, these notices are published only in VOEvent format through the GCN Kafka topics prefixed `gcn.notices.svom.voevent`.

Each of the 4 instruments have their dedicated topics to stream notices with their related information, see table below:

 <div className="overflow-table">

| <small> Kafka topic </small>                        | <small> Notice types </small>                                                            | <small> Latency </small>                          |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| <small> `gcn.notices.svom.voevent.grm` </small>     | <small> `grm-trigger` </small>                                                           | <small> ~15 sec </small>                          |
| <small> `gcn.notices.svom.voevent.eclairs` </small> | <small> `eclairs-wakeup` <br/>`eclairs-catalog`<br/ > `slewing` / `not-slewing` </small> | <small> ~15 sec <br/>~15 sec <br/> ~1 min</small> |

##### GRM notices

- the <small>`grm-trigger`</small> notice reports a detection of at least 2 out of the 3
  Gamma-Ray Detectors (GRDs) composing the GRM instrument. The localisation of the event with the GRM
  cannot be quickly retrieved, therefore <small>`grm-trigger`</small> notices can be issued without localisation information.
  Confirmation by another detection (e.g. ECLAIRs or another mission) may be necessary to localise
  the detection and organise a follow-up.

##### ECLAIRs notices

- the <small>`eclairs-wakeup`</small> notice reports a GRB candidate detection by ECLAIRs.
  The localisation typical uncertainty is about 8 arcmin.
- the <small>`eclairs-catalog`</small> notice reports a catalogued source detection by ECLAIRs.
- the <small>`slewing` / `not-slewing`</small> notice provides information on the platform slew status and might
  provide a better localisation with a typical uncertainty narrowed to about 6 arcmin:
  - a <small>`slewing`</small> notice contains the localization of the slew
    for an on-board follow-up by the MXT and VT instruments.
  - a <small>`not-slewing`</small> notice indicates that no slew will be performed by the platform.
    It describes whether the detection did not reach the slew threshold or the slew was not possible.

##### MXT notices

MXT notices are produced if MXT detects a source following a platform slew.
They are labelled according to the quality factor <small>`qf`</small> which indicates the accuracy
of the localisation: <small>`qf=4`</small> if R90 is smaller than 30 arcsec, <small>`qf=3`</small> if smaller than 60 arcsec,

<small>``qf=2``</small> if smaller than 120 arcsec, <small>``qf=1``</small> if
smaller than 300 arcsec.

They will be named as follow:
_ <small>`mxt-initial_qf#`</small> for the first detection
(<small>`#`</small> being the value of the quality factor),
_ <small>`mxt-update_qf#`</small> each time the quality factor
of the detection increases (for a maximum number of 4 MXT notices).
